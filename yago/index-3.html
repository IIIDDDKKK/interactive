ABOUT

Dena Yago (b. 1988, New York, NY) is an artist, writer, and strategist. Currently, she is the founder of How Soon Is Now, a consultancy for cultural, brand and social strategy and creative based in New York and Los Angeles.

Previously, she was a founder of the collective and trend forecasting group KHOLE (2011 - 2016). K-HOLE's free PDF trend reports on the nature of millennial change were downloaded half a million times, and have sparked countless media stories worldwide. Their term "normcore" was shortlisted for the Oxford English Dictionary's Word of the Year in 2014. K-HOLE's work has been presented at galleries and museums internationally, including the Serpentine Gallery and MoMA PS1.

As a strategist, Dena has worked on projects for Tesla, Hulu, Hulu’s The Handmaid’s Tale, Netflix, Fullscreen Media, Viacom, Los Angeles Philharmonic, and fashion brands such as Eckhaus Latta and Telfar Global.

Dena’s essays and interviews have appeared in publications including Texte zur Kunst, e-flux journal, 032c, Frieze Magazine, Garage Magazine, TANK Magazine and Flash Art International. She has been a visiting critic at the Yale School of Art since 2012, as well as a visiting artist at Universtiy of California Los Angeles, University of California San Diego, Otis College of Art and Design, and the School for Visual Arts.

Dena holds a B.A. in Visual Arts and German from Columbia University.
